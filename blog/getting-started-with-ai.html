开始之前，先回答三个问题 Before You Start, Answer These Three Questions

很多人上来就开始研究工具，结果研究了一圈发现自己不知道到底要做什么。磨刀不误砍柴工，先花五分钟想清楚下面三个问题，后面会顺畅很多。 Many people jump straight into researching tools, only to find after a while that they're not sure what they actually want to build. Sharpening the axe before chopping wood: take five minutes to think through these three questions, and everything after will go much more smoothly.

问题一：你想解决什么问题？ Question 1: What Problem Do You Want to Solve?

注意，不是"我想做个 App"——这种回答太抽象了，它不能指导你做任何具体的事情。正确的思考方式是：谁 + 遇到了什么困难 + 你想帮他怎么解决。 Note: "I want to build an app" is too abstract — it can't guide you to do anything specific. The right way to think about it is: who + what difficulty they face + how you want to help them solve it.

比如，不要说"我想做个菜谱 App"，要说"我妈妈每次想找上次做的红烧肉的配方都要翻半天手机相册，我想帮她做一个可以分类保存家庭菜谱的网页"。 For example, instead of saying "I want to build a recipe app," say "My mom spends ages scrolling through her phone photos every time she wants to find that braised pork recipe she made last time. I want to help her by building a webpage where she can save and categorize family recipes."

越具体越好。具体的问题才能导向具体的解决方案，模糊的想法只会让你在工具选择上迷失。 The more specific, the better. Specific problems lead to specific solutions. Vague ideas will only leave you lost in tool choices.

问题二：你的用户是谁？ Question 2: Who Is Your User?

用户画像越清晰，你做的产品就越有方向。不要想"所有人"——"所有人"等于"没有人"。你的用户可能只是你自己、你的家人、你的同事，或者是某个细分领域里的一小群人。 The clearer your user persona, the more focused your product will be. Don't think "everyone" — "everyone" means "no one." Your users might just be yourself, your family, your colleagues, or a small niche group in a specific domain.

举个例子：如果你是给 60 岁的妈妈做的菜谱工具，那界面要尽量大、操作要尽量简单、最好不需要注册登录。如果你是给程序员同事做的代码片段管理器，那功能可以更复杂，但效率必须高。用户是谁，直接决定了你用什么工具、做什么样的界面。 For example: if you're building a recipe tool for your 60-year-old mom, the interface should be large, interactions simple, and ideally no registration required. If you're building a code snippet manager for your programmer colleagues, features can be more complex, but efficiency must be high. Who your users are directly determines which tools you use and what kind of interface you build.

问题三：你能投入多少时间？ Question 3: How Much Time Can You Invest?

这个问题很现实但很重要。你打算每天花 30 分钟慢慢磨，还是这个周末集中两天全职投入？不同的时间预算，适合不同的工具和策略。 This is a practical but crucial question. Are you planning to spend 30 minutes a day chipping away slowly, or dedicate a full weekend (both days) to intensive work? Different time budgets suit different tools and strategies.

如果你只有碎片时间，建议用对话式 AI 工具（ChatGPT / Claude / 豆包）配合 AI 建站工具（v0.dev），因为这类工具上手快、反馈即时，每次打开都能看到进展。如果你有大块时间，可以考虑用 Cursor 或 Windsurf 这种 AI 编程工具，学习曲线稍陡但能做出更复杂的东西。 If you only have fragmented time, I recommend conversational AI tools (ChatGPT / Claude / Doubao) combined with AI website builders (v0.dev), because these tools have quick onboarding and instant feedback — every time you open them, you can see progress. If you have large blocks of time, consider AI coding tools like Cursor or Windsurf; the learning curve is slightly steeper but they can handle more complex projects.

AI 时代的工具地图：六个层级 The AI Era Tool Map: Six Levels

很多人觉得"AI 工具"就是 ChatGPT。其实 AI 工具已经发展成一个完整的生态系统，从想需求到做产品再到上线推广，每个环节都有对应的工具。下面我用六个层级帮你梳理清楚。 Many people think "AI tools" just means ChatGPT. In reality, AI tools have evolved into a complete ecosystem — from ideation to product building to launch and promotion, every step has corresponding tools. Below, I'll break it down into six levels for you.

1

对话式 AI：ChatGPT / Claude / 豆包 Conversational AI: ChatGPT / Claude / Doubao

用途：想清楚需求、写文案、做策划、头脑风暴。这是你接触 AI 的第一站。 Use case: Clarifying requirements, writing copy, planning, brainstorming. This is your first stop for AI.

在动手做任何产品之前，你应该先和 AI 对话，把你的想法聊清楚。很多时候你以为自己知道想要什么，其实并没有。比如你说"我想做一个效率工具"，AI 会追问你："什么样的效率？是时间管理？任务追踪？还是自动化处理？"这个对话过程本身就是极有价值的。 Before building anything, you should first talk to an AI and clarify your idea. Often, you think you know what you want, but you actually don't. For instance, you say "I want to build a productivity tool," and the AI follows up: "What kind of productivity? Time management? Task tracking? Or automation processing?" This conversation process itself is incredibly valuable.

中文用户推荐豆包（字节跳动的 AI 助手），对中文理解更好。英文场景推荐 Claude（Anthropic 出品，长文本理解能力强）或 ChatGPT（OpenAI 出品，生态最丰富）。 Chinese users should try Doubao (ByteDance's AI assistant) for better Chinese understanding. For English-language tasks, I recommend Claude (by Anthropic, strong at long-text comprehension) or ChatGPT (by OpenAI, the richest ecosystem).

用途：用自然语言描述需求，直接生成可运行的网页。零代码基础首选。 Use case: Describe your needs in natural language and get a runnable webpage directly. Best choice for zero coding background.

这是目前最适合非技术人的 AI 产品工具。你不需要安装任何软件，不需要打开终端，不需要懂 Git。打开浏览器，用中文或英文描述你想要的东西，它就会实时生成一个可以预览、可以交互的网页。 These are currently the most suitable AI product tools for non-technical people. You don't need to install any software, open a terminal, or understand Git. Open your browser, describe what you want in Chinese or English, and it generates a previewable, interactive webpage in real time.

Bolt.new（StackBlitz 出品）：支持更复杂的应用，可以生成带后端逻辑的全栈项目，在浏览器里就能运行。
Lovable：对自然语言理解能力最强，你可以像聊天一样逐步完善你的产品。 v0.dev (by Vercel): Best for single-page websites and landing pages. High generation quality, beautiful interfaces. Generated projects can be deployed directly to Vercel.
Bolt.new (by StackBlitz): Supports more complex applications, can generate full-stack projects with backend logic that run directly in the browser.

3

AI 编程工具：Cursor / Windsurf AI Coding Tools: Cursor / Windsurf

用途：需要稍微理解代码，但能做更复杂、更定制化的产品。 Use case: Requires a bit of code understanding, but can build more complex and customizable products.

当你发现 AI 建站工具已经不够用了——比如你需要用户登录、需要数据库、需要复杂的交互逻辑——这时候就该上 Cursor 或 Windsurf 了。它们是带有 AI 能力的代码编辑器，你用自然语言描述需求，AI 直接帮你写代码、改代码、管理项目。 When you find that AI website builders are no longer enough — for example, you need user authentication, a database, or complex interaction logic — that's when it's time to level up to Cursor or Windsurf. They're code editors with AI capabilities: describe your requirements in natural language, and the AI directly writes code, modifies code, and manages your project.

你不需要会写代码，但最好能看懂基本的代码结构（哪个文件是页面、哪个是样式、哪个是逻辑），这样和 AI 的沟通效率会更高。建议从 AI 建站工具入手，熟悉了基本概念后再过渡到这一层。 You don't need to write code, but it helps to understand basic code structure (which file is the page, which is the stylesheet, which is the logic) — this makes communication with the AI much more efficient. I recommend starting with AI website builders and transitioning to this level once you're familiar with the basic concepts.

部署工具：Vercel / GitHub Pages / Netlify Deployment Tools: Vercel / GitHub Pages / Netlify

用途：一键把你的产品放到互联网上，让任何人都能访问。 Use case: One-click publish your product to the internet so anyone can access it.

产品做出来了，当然要让别人看到。"部署"听起来很技术，但其实已经变得极其简单了。你只需要有一个 GitHub 账号（免费注册），然后把项目关联上去。 Once your product is built, naturally you want others to see it. "Deployment" sounds technical, but it has become incredibly simple. You just need a GitHub account (free to register), then connect your project.

Netlify：功能全面，适合各种类型的项目，操作也很直观。 Vercel: Most recommended, especially for v0.dev and Next.js projects. Once connected to GitHub, it automatically redeploys every time you update code.

适合：不会写代码、不想学代码、只想尽快做出一个能用的东西。 For: Those who can't code, don't want to learn to code, and just want to build something usable as fast as possible.

路径：v0.dev 生成界面 → 一键部署到 Vercel → 小红书分享 Path: Generate interface with v0.dev → One-click deploy to Vercel → Share on Xiaohongshu

预估时间：一个周末（如果需求简单，甚至一天就够） Estimated time: One weekend (or even just one day if requirements are simple)

优点：全程在浏览器里完成，不需要安装任何软件。上手极快，反馈即时。生成的页面质量已经很不错了。 Pros: Everything happens in the browser — no software installation needed. Extremely fast onboarding, instant feedback. The generated page quality is already quite good.

局限：适合单页面、相对简单的产品。如果你需要用户系统、数据库、复杂的业务逻辑，这条路径可能不够用。 Limitations: Suitable for single-page, relatively simple products. If you need user systems, databases, or complex business logic, this path may not be enough.

优点：能做出更复杂的产品，灵活性更高。Cursor 的 AI 能力很强，即使你只会一点点代码，也能做出超出你能力的产品。 Pros: Can build more complex products with higher flexibility. Cursor's AI is powerful enough that even with minimal coding knowledge, you can build products beyond your normal ability.

建议：如果选这条路，建议先跟着这篇教程安装 Cursor，走一遍完整流程。有了第一次成功经验后，之后做任何项目都会得心应手。 Tip: If you choose this path, I recommend first following the Cursor tutorial to install it and walk through a complete workflow. Once you have that first successful experience, every future project will feel natural.

路径 C：想认真做产品 Path C: Serious About Building Products

第一个项目做什么？三个适合新手的方向 What to Build First? Three Beginner-Friendly Directions

如果你还没有明确的想法，下面三个方向特别适合作为第一个项目。它们的共同特点是：需求明确、技术复杂度低、你自己就是用户（这意味着你有天然的测试反馈）。 If you don't have a clear idea yet, these three directions are especially suitable for a first project. They share common traits: clear requirements, low technical complexity, and you are your own user (meaning you have natural testing feedback).

个人工具类 Personal Utility Tools

比如：习惯打卡器、每日计划表、饮水提醒 Examples: habit tracker, daily planner, water intake reminder

  • 为什么适合：你自己就是用户，需求你最清楚 Why it's suitable: You are your own user — you know the requirements best
  • 技术复杂度：低，用 localStorage 存数据即可 Technical complexity: Low — localStorage is sufficient for data storage
  • 推荐工具：v0.dev 或 Cursor Recommended tools: v0.dev or Cursor
  • 典型提示词："我想做一个每天打卡的习惯追踪器，可以添加习惯名称、每天点一下打卡、显示连续打卡天数" Sample prompt: "I want to build a daily habit tracker where I can add habit names, tap to check in daily, and see streak counts"

内容生成类 Content Generation Tools

比如：文案助手、社交媒体帖子生成器、周报自动生成 Examples: copywriting assistant, social media post generator, weekly report auto-generator

  • 为什么适合：利用 AI 的文字能力，技术实现简单 Why it's suitable: Leverages AI's text capabilities; technically simple to implement
  • 技术复杂度：中低，需要调用 AI API（但 Cursor 能帮你搞定） Technical complexity: Low-medium — requires calling an AI API (but Cursor can handle this for you)
  • 推荐工具：Cursor（需要 API 集成） Recommended tools: Cursor (requires API integration)
  • 典型提示词："做一个文案助手页面，输入产品名称和卖点，自动生成适合小红书风格的种草文案" Sample prompt: "Build a copywriting assistant page where you input a product name and selling points, and it auto-generates Xiaohongshu-style promotional copy"

信息整理类 Information Organization Tools

比如：读书笔记整理器、收藏夹管理、播客笔记 Examples: book note organizer, bookmark manager, podcast notes

  • 为什么适合：解决真实痛点，使用频率高 Why it's suitable: Solves a real pain point with high usage frequency
  • 技术复杂度：低，核心就是增删改查 Technical complexity: Low — core functionality is just CRUD (create, read, update, delete)
  • 推荐工具：v0.dev 或 Cursor Recommended tools: v0.dev or Cursor
  • 典型提示词："做一个读书笔记整理器，可以添加书名、作者、摘抄和读后感，支持搜索和按分类筛选" Sample prompt: "Build a book note organizer where I can add book title, author, excerpts, and reviews, with search and category filtering"

24 小时挑战：从想法到上线 The 24-Hour Challenge: From Idea to Launch

如果你已经有了想法（或者从上面三个方向选了一个），下面是一个紧凑但可行的 24 小时计划。你可以一口气做完，也可以分两三天完成。关键是一气呵成，不要中途停下来"研究"。 If you already have an idea (or picked one from the three directions above), here's a tight but achievable 24-hour plan. You can do it in one sitting or spread it over two to three days. The key is momentum — don't stop to "research" in the middle.

1

0-2 小时：想清楚你要做什么 0-2 Hours: Clarify What You Want to Build

打开 ChatGPT、Claude 或豆包，把你的想法告诉它，让它帮你聊清楚。这一步的目标不是写代码，而是写一份产品描述——包括产品名称、核心功能、目标用户、界面风格。 Open ChatGPT, Claude, or Doubao, tell it your idea, and let it help you think it through. The goal of this step isn't to write code — it's to produce a product description including product name, core features, target users, and interface style. 

我想做一个习惯打卡网页应用。帮我想清楚：
1. 核心功能是什么？（除了打卡还能有什么）
2. 界面应该长什么样？
3. 适合什么名字？
4. 有什么类似的产品可以参考？

请帮我写一份完整的产品描述。I want to build a habit-tracking web app. Help me think through:
1. What are the core features? (Beyond just check-ins, what else?)
2. What should the interface look like?
3. What would be a good name?
4. Are there similar products I can reference?

心态比工具更重要 Mindset Matters More Than Tools

在你开始之前，我想分享三句话。这些不是什么鸡汤——它们是我在帮助很多非技术朋友上手 AI 产品后总结出来的真实经验。 Before you begin, I want to share three thoughts. These aren't platitudes — they are real lessons I've learned from helping many non-technical friends start building with AI.

完成比完美重要 Done Is Better Than Perfect

你的第一个产品不会是完美的。它可能有 bug，界面可能不够好看，功能可能不够完善。没关系。全世界最成功的产品的第一个版本也都是粗糙的。Facebook 的第一个版本只是一个 Harvard 校内的简陋交友页面。Airbnb 的第一个版本就是几个气垫床的照片。完美主义是行动的天敌——先做一个 60 分的产品，再迭代到 80 分，比你一直在脑子里规划一个 100 分的产品要有效一万倍。 Your first product won't be perfect. It might have bugs, the interface might not be beautiful enough, features might not be complete. That's okay. The first version of every successful product in the world was rough. Facebook's first version was just a barebones Harvard-only social page. Airbnb's first version was literally photos of air mattresses. Perfectionism is the enemy of action — building a 60-point product and iterating to 80 is ten thousand times more effective than endlessly planning a 100-point product in your head.

第一个产品不会是最后一个 Your First Product Won't Be Your Last

把第一个项目当成"练手"。你从中学到的经验——怎么和 AI 沟通、怎么部署、怎么修 bug——这些才是真正的收获。你做的第一个产品可能没人用，但它给你打开了一扇门。有了这扇门，你的第二个产品会好十倍，第三个产品会好百倍。我见过太多人因为第一个项目不够好就放弃了，其实第一个项目存在的意义就是让你"学会做项目"，而不是"做一个完美的项目"。 Treat your first project as "practice." The real takeaways are the skills you learn — how to communicate with AI, how to deploy, how to fix bugs. Your first product might not have any users, but it opens a door for you. With that door open, your second product will be ten times better, and your third will be a hundred times better. I've seen too many people give up because their first project wasn't good enough. In reality, the purpose of the first project is to "learn how to build," not to "build something perfect."

享受过程，不要焦虑 Enjoy the Process, Don't Stress

社交媒体上到处都是"我用 AI 3 天做了一个月入万刀的 SaaS"的故事——请理性看待。这些故事要么夸大了，要么省略了背后大量的积累。你的节奏是自己的，不需要和任何人比较。享受用 AI 把想法变成现实的过程本身，这就已经是一件很酷的事了。5 年前，能自己做产品的人凤毛麟角；今天，你只需要会说话就能做。这本身就是一个奇迹。 Social media is full of "I built a $10K/month SaaS with AI in 3 days" stories — please view these rationally. These stories are either exaggerated or omit a massive amount of accumulated experience behind the scenes. Your pace is your own — you don't need to compare with anyone. Enjoy the process of turning ideas into reality with AI. That alone is already a remarkable thing. Five years ago, people who could build their own products were rare as hen's teeth; today, you only need to know how to speak to build. That itself is a miracle.
