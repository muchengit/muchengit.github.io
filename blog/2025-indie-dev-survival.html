2025 年的独立开发现状 The State of Indie Development in 2025

2025 年的独立开发圈有两个极端的声音。一边是"AI 让人人都能做产品"的乐观叙事，另一边是"独立开发已死"的悲观论调。真相在中间：AI 确实大幅降低了做产品的门槛，但做出产品和做出赚钱的产品是两回事。门槛降低了意味着竞争更激烈，竞争更激烈意味着你需要更聪明地做选择。 The indie dev community in 2025 has two extreme narratives. One is the optimistic "AI lets everyone build products," the other is the pessimistic "indie dev is dead." The truth is in between: AI has indeed dramatically lowered the barrier to building, but building a product and building a profitable product are two different things. Lower barriers mean fiercer competition, and fiercer competition means you need to make smarter choices.

这篇文章基于我自己的真实经历。过去一年，我用 AI 辅助做了 8 个产品——2 个赚钱、4 个不赚不亏、2 个血亏。我会把每个产品的盈亏数据都公开出来，并从中提炼出对新独立开发者的生存建议。 This article is based on my real experience. Over the past year, I built 8 products with AI assistance — 2 profitable, 4 break-even, 2 total losses. I'll disclose the P&L data for each product and extract survival advice for new indie developers.

成功 vs 失败：关键差异 Success vs Failure: Key Differences

成功产品特征Successful Product Traits

解决一个具体的小痛点，而非大而全的平台。开发时间控制在 2 周以内。从第一天就有清晰的变现模式。面向明确的付费用户群（而非"所有人"）。有现成的获客渠道（Reddit、Product Hunt、SEO）。 Solves one specific small pain point, not a comprehensive platform. Development time under 2 weeks. Clear monetization from day one. Targets a defined paying user group (not "everyone"). Has existing acquisition channels (Reddit, Product Hunt, SEO).

失败产品特征Failed Product Traits

试图做"更好的 XXX"，但和已有竞品差异不够大。开发时间超过 3 周。没有想清楚怎么赚钱就开工。面向模糊的用户群（"所有上班族""所有学生"）。没有获客计划，以为"做出来就会有人用"。 Tried to build a "better XXX" but wasn't different enough from existing competitors. Development time exceeded 3 weeks. Started building without a clear monetization plan. Targeted vague user groups ("all office workers" "all students"). No acquisition plan, assumed "build it and they'll come."

独立开发者 5 条生存法则 5 Survival Rules for Indie Developers

1

两周一刀：控制开发时间 The Two-Week Rule: Control Dev Time

我的两个赚钱产品（AI 简历优化器和 Notion 模板商城）开发时间分别是 2 周和 1 周。两个血亏的产品分别花了 4 周和 2 周。规律很清楚：开发时间越长，失败概率越高。因为开发时间越长意味着你投入的成本越大，对"一定能成"的执念越深，就越不愿意放弃一个本该放弃的方向。给自己设一个硬性deadline：任何产品最多开发两周，超时就上线，行不行让用户告诉你。 My two profitable products (AI Resume Optimizer and Notion Template Shop) took 2 weeks and 1 week respectively. The two losing products took 4 weeks and 2 weeks. The pattern is clear: longer development = higher failure probability. Because longer dev means more sunk cost, stronger "it must work" conviction, and more reluctance to abandon a direction you should. Set a hard deadline: any product gets at most two weeks of dev time. If time's up, ship it — let users tell you if it works.

2

先找用户，再写代码 Find Users Before Writing Code

AI 简历优化器的想法来自我在招聘论坛上看到大量"简历怎么写"的求助帖。我先在论坛里回复了几十个人的简历问题，确认了这个需求确实存在、而且人们愿意付费，才开始做产品。而 AI 编程助手是我自己"觉得有需求"就做的——结果 0 用户。需求验证不值得跳过，它只需要 1-2 天，但能帮你省下几周的无效劳动。 The AI Resume Optimizer idea came from seeing tons of "how to write a resume" help requests on job forums. I first replied to dozens of people's resume questions on forums, confirmed the demand existed and people would pay, then started building. The AI Coding Assistant was something I "felt there was demand for" — result: 0 users. Demand validation is not worth skipping. It takes 1-2 days but saves you weeks of wasted effort.

3

小而美，不要大而全 Small and Beautiful, Not Big and Comprehensive

AI 社交媒体管理工具失败的最大原因是我想做得太多：自动发帖、自动回复、数据分析、竞品监控……一个独立开发者根本做不过 Hootsuite 和 Buffer。而我赚钱的 AI 简历优化器只做一件事：帮你优化简历。一个功能做到极致，比十个功能都做半成品强一万倍。 The biggest reason the AI Social Media Management tool failed was I tried to do too much: auto-posting, auto-reply, analytics, competitor monitoring... A solo dev can't compete with Hootsuite and Buffer. But my profitable AI Resume Optimizer only does one thing: optimize your resume. One feature done exceptionally well is ten thousand times better than ten half-baked features.

4

快速试错，快速放弃 Fail Fast, Kill Fast

AI 编程助手上线两周后只有 5 个下载，其中 3 个是我自己。这时候就应该放弃，但我又花了一周去"优化"——毫无意义。正确的做法是：设定一个"死亡指标"，比如上线两周 DAU 不到 10 就放弃。到了指标就砍，不要犹豫。放弃不是失败，在不值得的事情上浪费时间才是真正的失败。 The AI Coding Assistant had only 5 downloads after two weeks of launch, 3 of which were me. I should have killed it then, but spent another week "optimizing" — completely pointless. The right approach: set a "kill metric," like if DAU is under 10 after two weeks of launch, kill it. When the metric triggers, cut it without hesitation. Killing a product isn't failure — wasting time on something not worth it is the real failure.

5

把时间花在分发上，而非开发上 Spend Time on Distribution, Not Development

复盘我的时间分配，赚钱的两个产品大约 40% 时间开发、60% 时间做分发（写博客、发 Reddit、做 SEO、联系 KOL）。而失败的产品大约 90% 时间在开发，几乎没有做分发。产品做得再好，没人看到就等于零。独立开发者最容易犯的错误就是过度沉迷于开发，而忽略了获客。建议的时间分配：30% 开发，50% 分发，20% 维护和迭代。 Reviewing my time allocation, the two profitable products spent ~40% time on development and 60% on distribution (blogging, Reddit posting, SEO, reaching out to KOLs). The failing products spent ~90% on development, almost zero on distribution. No matter how good the product, if nobody sees it, it equals zero. The easiest mistake for indie devs is getting addicted to development while ignoring acquisition. Recommended time split: 30% development, 50% distribution, 20% maintenance and iteration.

核心结论：独立开发不是赌博。虽然结果有不确定性，但你可以通过控制变量来大幅提高成功率。控制开发时间（两周）、先验证需求、只做一个功能、设定死亡指标、重视分发——这五条法则不能保证你一定成功，但能帮你避免我踩过的坑。2025 年的 AI 独立开发，聪明比勤奋重要，选择比努力重要，放弃比坚持重要。 Core conclusion: Indie dev isn't gambling. While outcomes are uncertain, you can dramatically improve success rates by controlling variables. Limit dev time (two weeks), validate demand first, build only one feature, set kill metrics, prioritize distribution — these five rules can't guarantee success, but they'll help you avoid the traps I fell into. In 2025 AI indie dev, being smart matters more than being hardworking, choosing matters more than trying, and quitting matters more than persisting.
